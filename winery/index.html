Scheduling Rules & Heuristics

The auto-scheduler uses the following rules in order. Understanding them helps you predict its output and use manual overrides effectively.

Availability
1
Availability Gating
An employee is never scheduled on a day they marked unavailable in the Google Sheet. Only days are tracked — there is no per-shift availability. If an employee has no sheet data at all, they are treated as available every day (benefit of the doubt).
2
Sheet Format
The Google Sheet has one row per employee and one column per date (ISO format: YYYY-MM-DD). Any non-blank mark in a cell means available — employees just mark days they can work and leave the rest blank. Explicit "N", "NO", or "FALSE" also work as unavailable markers.
Staffing Requirements
3
Manager Per Shift
Every shift (A and B) must have at least one Manager. If no Manager is available for a shift, that shift is flagged with a warning badge on the calendar and day detail view. The shift is still filled with available Wine Pourers — it is not left empty.
4
Target Staffing Level
Each shift is filled up to the "Target staff per shift" number configured in Settings (default: 2). The scheduler does not exceed this number unless you add staff manually.
5
One Assignment Per Day
An employee is assigned to at most one shift per day. Once placed on Shift A, they are excluded from Shift B that same day, and vice versa. This applies to both auto-assigned and manually-assigned staff.
6
Shift Identity
Each employee can clearly see whether they are on Shift A (11:30 AM – 5:00 PM) or Shift B (12:30 PM – 6:00 PM) by viewing the Day Detail screen. Shift assignments are shown on separate labelled cards.
Fairness & Distribution
7
Fairness Score
Employees are ranked by how many days they have worked in the past four weeks. Those with fewer recent days get scheduled first. Shift A and Shift B on the same day count as one day worked — the specific shift does not affect the fairness score.
8
Recency Weighting
Days worked in the most recent week of the lookback window count 3× more than days from earlier weeks. This prevents an employee who just had a busy stretch from being immediately over-scheduled again in the next run.
9
Manager Priority Within a Shift
Among all available candidates for a shift, one Manager is always seated first. Remaining slots are filled with Wine Pourers (sorted by fairness score). If more slots remain after all Wine Pourers are placed, additional Managers fill them.
10
Random Tiebreaker
When two employees have identical fairness scores, a small random factor determines who is picked first. This prevents the same person from always being chosen when scores are tied (e.g. two new employees with zero history).
Manual Overrides
11
Manual Override Protection
If a shift contains any manually-assigned staff member (marked with a ✎ indicator), the auto-scheduler skips that shift entirely on the next run. Manual assignments are never overwritten. Use Clear Auto to reset only auto-assigned slots while keeping all manual assignments intact.
12
Manual Assignments and Fairness
Manually-assigned shifts count toward an employee's fairness score in future scheduling runs, the same as auto-assigned shifts. Manually placing someone does not give them an advantage in subsequent months.
