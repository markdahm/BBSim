Rules applied by the auto-scheduler in order. Understanding them helps predict results and use manual edits effectively.

Availability

1
Day-Level Availability Gating
Employees are never scheduled on days they marked unavailable in the Availability tab. Only days matter — there is no per-shift availability. Any non-blank mark in a cell means available; blank means unavailable.
2
No Data = Available
If an employee has no row in the Availability tab at all, they are treated as available every day (benefit of the doubt). Add them to Availability to restrict their days.

Staffing Requirements

3
Manager Per Shift
Every shift (A and B) must have at least one Manager. If no Manager is available, that shift is highlighted orange as a warning. The shift is still filled with available Wine Pourers — it is not left empty.
4
Target Staffing Level
Each shift is filled up to the Target Staff Per Shift value in Settings (default: 2). The scheduler never exceeds this number.
5
One Assignment Per Day
Each employee is assigned to at most one shift per day. Once placed on Shift A, they are excluded from Shift B the same day, and vice versa.
6
Shift Identity
Employees can see whether they are on Shift A (11:30 AM – 5:00 PM) or Shift B (12:30 PM – 6:00 PM) in the Schedule tab. Each shift has its own column with the time clearly labelled.

Fairness & Distribution

7
Fairness Score
Employees are ranked by days worked in the past four weeks. Those with fewer recent days are scheduled first. Shift A and Shift B on the same day count as one day — the specific shift does not affect fairness.
8
Recency Weighting
Days worked in the most recent 7 days count 3× more than earlier days. This prevents someone who just had a busy week from being immediately over-scheduled again.
9
Manager Priority Within a Shift
One Manager is always seated first within a shift. Remaining slots are filled with Wine Pourers (sorted by fairness). If more slots remain after all Wine Pourers are placed, additional Managers fill them.
10
Random Tiebreaker
When two employees have identical fairness scores, a small random factor decides who is picked. This prevents alphabetical or positional bias among equally-eligible staff.

Manual Overrides

11
Re-running the Scheduler
Running Auto-Schedule Month always generates a fresh schedule for the whole month. Any manual edits you made to the Schedule tab will be overwritten. Make manual tweaks after the auto-run, not before.
12
Manual Edits Count Toward Fairness
Edits you make to the Schedule tab after the auto-run are not tracked in History automatically. If you frequently place the same person manually, consider running the scheduler again so it can re-balance around them.
